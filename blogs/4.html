Developer Diary

February 4th, 2022

Controller API

Our prof showed us his app that can detect and then print out all of a controller's events whenever he pushes a button or used the joystick. He briefly showed us the code for his program and tasked us with incorporating the controller into our game. It's the midterm week coming up and we still hadn't worked on a single line of code together for the game, I couldn't help but feel a little bit worried about not finishing this game in time.

Team Retrospective

At the end of the week, the chat team had a sprint retrospective and the 'Run game' team evaluated the members of the group. For the chat protocol, our group were able to finish most of the important tasks for this sprint, the protocol examples are the only ones that got pushed back to the backlog for the next sprint. On On the other hand, the Run game's team had a little bit of an argument since some of the more vocal and active members in the team felt quite unfair that they have to carry the workload sometimes since the more reserved members are not contributing as much. We collectively decided that everyone should take turns and voice their opinion from the next meeting onwards.
